Every now and then, there is software that bugs you. Like really bugs you. Take Windows Vista for example or Norton Anti Virus, even Xcode. Usually there’s alternatives for them or they eventually get upgraded to address some of the annoyances, so you can cool down after a while. Then there’s Blackboard.

The title may seem hyperbolic but I seriously believe these words. Blackboard is one of the worst pieces of software I have ever encountered and I’m not alone either (this rant is particulary eloquent, definitely worth the read). For those fortunately enough to be unfamiliar with Blackboard, it’s an CMS for educational institutions. My university, NUI Galway, uses it to manage it’s courses and modules. Most of the lecturers upload their content online such as lectures and assignments. It’s an application I have to use daily for the past two years so I’ve come quite accustomed to it. The purpose of this post is to go through some of the “annoyances” or features/procedures that the Jigsaw himself devised with Blackboard. This may seem like a whiny monologue from a disgruntled student however I build software myself so noticing these things is part of the job.

Where to start.

How about the literal start, the login page. Here were greeted with a dialog that pops up every fucking visit which you have to agree to use the site.

Next, how about the frames. The “navigation” and content are split into two frames. The navigation being the controller for the content frame. How long ago were frames considered bad practice? Blackboard was created in 1997[1].

Frames were definitely in vogue then however now, 17 years later,there is AJAX, a much more stable, extensible and widely accepted alternative. It’s not like they don’t know about AJAX though, pop open the console and you can see plenty of XHR requests yet they still persist with their frames. Incredible. Maybe it’s down to their theming functionality but at what point do you stop supporting legacy code, Blackboard is at version 9.

Onto the navigation. In the top right hand corner is four buttons. One for logout which is probably one of the only buttons that did what I assumed it would do (the off button). Next we have two buttons which do exactly the same thing or at least, nothing noticeably different. Each bring you to a page

which display your courses. Next we have the dropdown menu which seems like it does a lot, but in reality it just vomits up an exact UI replica into the content frame.
